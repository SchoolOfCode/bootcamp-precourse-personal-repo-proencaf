Quokkas

The World's Happiest Animal

Here's some interesting facts about Quokkas

  1. Their scientific name is SETONIX BRACHYURUS
  2. The primary home for Quokkas is on Rottnest Island however small groups also inhabit mainland WA as well as Bald Island, Australia
  3. Quokkas were first discovered in 1696 by Willem de Vlamingh who mistakenly though they were giant rats. He named the island "Ratten-nest"
  4. They are part of the kangaroo family and move around by hopping
  5. Quokkas can survive a long time without food and water by storing fat in their tails
  6. They weigh between 2.5 to 5 kilograms, and are rougly the same of a small cat
  7. In the wild they can live up to 15 years
  8. Baby quokkas are called 'joeys' and when born, they ride in their mother's pouch for about six months
  9. On the mainland, quokkas can breed all year around but on Rottnest Island breeding season is from January to August
  10. It is estimated that there are between 7,500-15,000 mature adults in the wild
